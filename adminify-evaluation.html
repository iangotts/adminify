01

Business needs

Does this tool materially reduce your Salesforce admin workload — and can you prove it before you buy?

Core capability fit

  • Does the tool handle flows, data cleanup, user management, Apex, documentation, and reports?
  • Can it read your org's schema, custom objects, and third-party integrations from day one?
  • Does it cover the full stack including Apex triggers, batch jobs, and LWC — or declarative only?

Productivity and backlog impact

  • What is the measurable time saving per task category, backed by reference customer data?
  • Can it clear a weeks-long backlog in hours rather than days?
  • Does it free senior admins for strategic work, or just speed up tasks already done quickly?

Workflow integration

  • Does it fit into your existing change management, release, and approval processes?
  • Can it connect to your ticketing system (Jira, ServiceNow) to action tickets directly?
  • Does it work alongside Salesforce CLI, VS Code, and DevOps Centre — or replace them?

ROI and measurability

  • What is the cost per task compared to consultant day rates or staff time?
  • Can usage, time savings, and task volume be reported back to management?
  • How quickly does the tool reach payback against current tooling and headcount spend?
02

Security and access

AI tools that connect to your Salesforce org need to earn trust. Scrutinise every layer of the security model before connecting to production.

Authentication and authorisation

  • How does the tool authenticate with Salesforce: OAuth 2.0, named credentials, or a connected app?
  • What Salesforce permissions does the connected user require? Is least-privilege documented?
  • Can access be scoped to specific orgs, objects, or action types?

Data handling and residency

  • Does the tool store your org schema, field data, or metadata on its own servers?
  • Where is data stored, for how long, under what encryption, and in which region?
  • Is org data ever used to train or fine-tune underlying AI models?

Compliance certifications

  • Does the vendor hold SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR, HIPAA, or relevant sector certifications?
  • Are certifications current, independently audited, and available for review under NDA?
  • Can a data processing agreement (DPA) be provided for GDPR compliance?

Audit trail and rollback

  • Are all AI-generated changes logged with a full, immutable audit trail?
  • Can audit logs be exported or integrated with a SIEM or compliance tooling?
  • Is there a rollback mechanism, and how granular is it?
03

Platform

Validate technical depth and compatibility before committing. Surface-level demos rarely expose what happens on complex or legacy orgs.

Salesforce compatibility

  • Which clouds are supported: Sales, Service, Experience, Health, Financial Services, Revenue?
  • Does it support Classic, Lightning, or both?
  • Is it compatible with sandboxes, scratch orgs, and developer orgs — not just production?

Multi-org and enterprise scale

  • How many orgs are supported per plan, and what are the switching and comparison capabilities?
  • Is there a multi-org mode for managing many client environments or post-M&A consolidation?
  • Can it surface conflicts, data model misalignment, and permission issues across orgs?

Technical depth

  • Can it generate production-grade Apex — triggers, batch jobs, test classes, governor-safe patterns?
  • Does it understand Salesforce best practices and avoid common anti-patterns?
  • Can it handle complex metadata deployments including managed packages and namespaces?

Performance and reliability

  • What is the stated uptime SLA and where is the status page?
  • How does AI task quality hold up on large orgs with 1,000+ objects or millions of records?
  • Are there rate limits on AI tasks that could bottleneck a busy team during peak periods?
04

Product roadmap

In a fast-moving AI market, what a vendor ships in the next 12 months matters as much as what they have today.

Current completeness and GA status

  • Which features are generally available versus in beta, early access, or on the roadmap?
  • Are there capability gaps relevant to your use cases that the vendor commits to close?
  • How mature is Apex and LWC generation compared to declarative task handling?

Velocity and transparency

  • How frequently are new features shipped, and is there a public changelog?
  • Is the roadmap available for review, and can enterprise customers influence prioritisation?
  • How does the vendor respond to Salesforce platform changes and Agentforce developments?

AI model strategy

  • Which underlying models power the platform, and is the vendor dependent on a single provider?
  • What happens to output quality when the underlying model is updated or changed?
  • Is there model selection or fallback capability for sensitive or regulated environments?

Competitive durability

  • How does the roadmap respond to Salesforce's own AI investments — Einstein and Agentforce?
  • What is the long-term differentiation if Salesforce ships similar native capabilities?
  • Is there a vision beyond task automation — org intelligence, proactive risk detection?
05

Support

AI tooling that touches production Salesforce requires vendor support that matches the stakes.

Onboarding and time to value

  • How long does it take to connect an org and produce genuinely useful output — not just a demo?
  • Is there a guided onboarding flow with a dedicated customer success contact?
  • Are workflow templates, prompt libraries, or pre-built automations included?

Documentation and self-service

  • Is the documentation comprehensive, version-controlled, and searchable?
  • Are there worked examples for the specific admin tasks your team performs most?
  • Is there an active community forum, Slack, or user group for peer knowledge sharing?

Reactive support quality

  • What are the SLA response times by plan tier — community, email, Slack, dedicated?
  • Is support staffed by people with genuine Salesforce admin expertise?
  • How are AI errors — wrong output, failed deployments — handled and resolved?

Enterprise support options

  • Is a dedicated customer success manager available at enterprise tier?
  • Are professional services available for complex migrations or custom onboarding?
  • Can the vendor provide Salesforce architecture guidance alongside AI task execution?
06

Company background

You are giving this vendor write access to your Salesforce production org. The company must be evaluated with the same rigour as the product.

Founding team and credentials

  • Do the founders have deep Salesforce and AI expertise — not just general SaaS backgrounds?
  • Have they previously built, scaled, or worked at Salesforce ecosystem companies?
  • Is there a technical advisory board with recognised Salesforce or enterprise software names?

Funding and financial stability

  • Is the company venture-backed, bootstrapped, or part of a larger group?
  • What is the known funding round and lead investor — does it indicate institutional conviction?
  • Is there sufficient runway to support a 2–3 year vendor relationship without acquisition risk?

Salesforce ecosystem standing

  • Is the vendor a certified Salesforce ISV, consulting partner, or AppExchange listed?
  • Does it have co-marketing relationships, Dreamforce presence, or Salesforce Ventures backing?
  • Does it maintain access to Salesforce pre-release programs and early API capabilities?

Acquisition and continuity risk

  • Is the company positioned for acquisition — and what are the implications for your contract?
  • If the vendor shuts down, what is the data exit and migration path?
  • How dependent is the product on a single AI provider, and what is the contingency?
